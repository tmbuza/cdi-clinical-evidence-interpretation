# Clinical Evidence Interpretation for Medical Devices

**From Results to Defensible Clinical Claims**

---

## Overview

This guide provides a structured approach to interpreting clinical evidence for medical devices.

It focuses on a critical gap often seen in practice:

> Results are reported, but not always translated into defensible clinical claims.

This guide addresses that gap by introducing a clear reasoning framework that moves from:

**evidence → evaluation → interpretation → claims**

---

## What this guide covers

### Foundations
- Understanding clinical evidence  
- Defining intended use  
- Establishing the basis for evaluation  

### Core Evaluation Reasoning
- Study appraisal  
- Bias and limitations  
- Risk–benefit evaluation  
- Equivalence and justification  
- Clinical context and applicability  

### From Results to Claims
- Synthesis and interpretation  
- Translating results into defensible claims  
- Writing clear, justifiable statements  

### Practice and Closure
- Common failure points  
- Summary and practical application  

---

## Key principle

> **Defensible clinical claims emerge from structured interpretation, not isolated results.**

---

## How to use this guide

- Follow chapters sequentially for full understanding  
- Apply the Appendix checklists during evaluation  
- Use the framework on real clinical evidence
